# SQL Marketing Analytics Case Study

## Overview

End-to-end SQL project simulating a real-world marketing analytics environment.  
Analyzed campaign performance, customer behavior, and revenue impact using structured queries on a dataset of:

- 1,000 customers  
- 10 marketing campaigns  
- 9,000+ interactions  
- Paid vs Organic attribution  
- Churn and repeat purchase behavior  

---

## Business Questions Addressed

- What is the ROI of each marketing campaign?
- What is the Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)?
- Which customers generate the highest Lifetime Value (CLV)?
- Do Paid campaigns outperform Organic campaigns?
- How does churn impact total revenue?
- Are there seasonal revenue trends?

---

## SQL Skills Demonstrated

- JOINs (INNER, LEFT)
- GROUP BY & Aggregations (SUM, COUNT, AVG)
- DISTINCT & CASE logic
- Date functions & cohort analysis
- Recursive CTE for large-scale data generation
- Indexing for performance optimization

---

## Key Insights

- Identified profitable vs underperforming campaigns using ROI analysis  
- Measured acquisition efficiency through CAC calculations  
- Discovered that a small segment of repeat buyers drives disproportionate revenue  
- Observed seasonal revenue spikes 
- Compared revenue contribution between churned and active customers
